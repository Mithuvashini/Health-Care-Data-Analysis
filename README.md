## Dashboard Overview – Healthcare Dashboard (AI Analysis)

The dashboard provides a consolidated view of healthcare patterns by combining clinical, demographic, and financial metrics. Interactive slicers enable dynamic filtering across multiple dimensions.

---

## Dashboard Filters (Slicers)
- Cancer History (Yes / No)
- Smoker (Yes / No)
- Weight Status (Underweight, Normalweight, Overweight, Obesity)
- Diabetes Status (Normal, Prediabetes, Diabetes)
- Number of Major Surgeries (0–3)

These slicers allow focused analysis across specific patient segments.

---

## Visual Analysis & Insights

### 1. Cancer History vs Smoker & Non-Smoker
**Chart Type:** Clustered Column Chart  

- Non-smokers show a significantly higher population count overall.
- Cancer occurrence is notably higher among smokers compared to non-smokers when viewed proportionally.
- Indicates smoking as a strong contributing risk factor.

---

### 2. Diabetes Status vs Average Age
**Chart Type:** Horizontal Bar Chart  

- Normal individuals average ~38 years.
- Prediabetic individuals average ~37 years.
- Diabetic individuals show a much higher average age (~52 years).
- Suggests age as a strong indicator in diabetes progression.

---

### 3. Surgery Count vs Average Age
**Chart Type:** Line Chart  

- Average age increases with the number of major surgeries.
- Patients with 3 surgeries have the highest average age (~65 years).
- Indicates cumulative medical complexity with aging.

---

### 4. Weight Status vs Charges
**Chart Type:** Pie Chart  

- Obesity accounts for the highest proportion of healthcare charges (~64%).
- Overweight contributes ~18%.
- Normal weight contributes ~14%.
- Underweight contributes minimal cost (~4%).
- Strong correlation between higher BMI and healthcare expenditure.

---

### 5. Hospital Tier vs Charges
**Chart Type:** Donut Chart  

- Tier-2 hospitals account for the highest total charges.
- Tier-1 hospitals follow, indicating higher-cost treatments.
- Tier-3 hospitals contribute comparatively lower charges.
- Highlights cost concentration in mid- and top-tier hospitals.

---

### 6. City Tier vs Customer Count
**Chart Type:** Area Chart  

- Tier-2 cities show the highest customer count.
- Tier-1 cities have lower patient volume.
- Tier-3 cities show moderate but consistent engagement.
- Suggests higher healthcare access/utilization in Tier-2 cities.

---

## Key Business Insights
- Smoking and obesity are major cost and risk drivers.
- Age strongly correlates with diabetes and surgical complexity.
- Tier-2 hospitals and cities represent the highest utilization zones.
- Preventive care targeting obesity and smoking can significantly reduce healthcare costs.

---

## Dashboard Purpose
This dashboard is designed to:
- Enable quick clinical and financial insights
- Support healthcare decision-making
- Combine manual Excel analytics with AI-assisted visual analysis
